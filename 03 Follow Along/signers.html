Who were the signers

Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration. Later became the 3rd President of the United States and founded the University of Virginia.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Strong advocate for independence; helped draft the Declaration. Became the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Diplomat and elder statesman; helped revise the Declaration. Also a renowned inventor and writer.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress. Famous for his large and stylish signature.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Influential leader in the independence movement and cousin of John Adams. Helped organize the Boston Tea Party.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the resolution for independence in the Continental Congress.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer and the last surviving signer (died in 1832).

These individuals were not only signers of the Declaration of Independence, but also key figures in shaping the foundation of the United States. Their courage, leadership, and vision continue to influence the nation's values and democratic principles to this day.
